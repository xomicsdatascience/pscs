About PSCS

{% endblock %} {% block content %}

The Platform for Single-Cell Science (PSCS, "pai-seez") is a system developed for methods related to Single Cell with the following goals:

  • Improving accessibility of analytical methods.
  • Ensuring reproducibility of results by making version control and containerization transparent.
  • Linking published results with results verified by a third party.
  • Facilitating multi-site collaboration with analyses that can be fully inspected.
  • Associating results with permanent DOI.
  • Providing data hosting for promoting science.

Acknowledgement

PSCS is being developed by the XOmics Data Science Lab headed by Dr. Jesse Meyer. The lab is associated with the department of Computational Biomedicine at Cedars Sinai. Distributed computation is made possible by services provided by the OSG Consortium [1-3], which is supported by the National Science Foundation awards #2030508 and #1836650.
